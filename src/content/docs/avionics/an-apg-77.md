The **AN/APG-77** is a solid-state, active electronically scanned array (AESA) radar installed on the F-22 Raptor. It is the primary sensor for the aircraft, providing unparalleled situational awareness, low probability of intercept (LPI) operation, and advanced electronic warfare capabilities.

## Specifications

- **Manufacturer:** Northrop Grumman and Lockheed Martin
- **Type:** Active Electronically Scanned Array (AESA)
- **Range:** Estimated at 120-150 miles (for a 1 m² target)
- **Modules:** Approx. 1,956 transmit/receive (TR) modules

## Low Probability of Intercept (LPI)

A key feature of the APG-77 is its LPI capability. The radar system achieves this by:
1. Emitting pulses across a wide frequency band.
2. Rapidly changing frequencies to prevent hostile radar warning receivers from locking on.
3. Keeping emissions narrow and specifically targeted.

> [!NOTE]
> When operating in LPI mode, the F-22 can track and target enemy aircraft without alerting them that they are being painted by radar.

## Electronic Warfare (EW)

Beyond traditional tracking, the high power and agility of the AESA modules allow the radar to function as a directed energy weapon, capable of overloading hostile sensors or executing pinpoint cyber-attacks against enemy data links.
