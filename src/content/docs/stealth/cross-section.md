---
title: "Radar Cross-Section (RCS)"
description: "Analysis of the F-22's stealth characteristics."
---

The **Radar Cross-Section (RCS)** of an aircraft determines how easily it can be detected by radar. The F-22 Raptor was designed from the ground up to minimize its RCS, making it nearly invisible to modern air defense networks.

## Design Principles

The Raptor's stealth is achieved through a combination of physical shaping and advanced materials:

- **Planform Alignment:** The edges of the wings, tail surfaces, and intake ramps are precisely aligned to reflect radar waves away from the receiver.
- **Internal Weapons Carriage:** Unlike legacy fighters that carry munitions on external pylons, the F-22 houses its missiles internally to maintain a clean aerodynamic and radar profile.
- **Radar-Absorbent Material (RAM):** The aircraft is coated with classified materials that absorb rather than reflect electromagnetic energy.

## Estimated RCS

While exact figures are classified, the F-22's RCS is estimated to be roughly the size of a **steel marble** (approximately 0.0001 m²) when viewed from the front.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Stealth is not invisibility. Detection depends on the radar's power, wavelength, and the angle of the aircraft relative to the sensor. The F-22's stealth is optimized against high-frequency targeting radars (X-band).
